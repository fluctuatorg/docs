At Xendit, we take security seriously because protecting your business is paramount. We employ robust measures like Two-factor Authentication (2FA), granular user permissions, and secure API keys to safeguard your account and sensitive information.

Here's why account security is important:

- Prevents unauthorized access: Malicious actors could gain access to your data or make payments using your Xendit account.
- Maintains trust with customers: Customers expect their payment information to be safe.

## How you can optimize your security

- Two-factor authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security by requiring a code from your phone in addition to your password.
- User permissions: Control who can access different parts of your Xendit account.
- API Keys: Restrict access to your Xendit account through APIs.
- Secure integration practices: Protect your integration by employing integration security best practices.

Follow these best practices to further enhance your account security:

- Use a strong password: Create a password that is at least 8 characters long and includes a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
- Don't share your password with anyone: This includes writing it down or storing it electronically in an insecure way.
- Be cautious of phishing emails: These emails try to trick you into revealing your personal information.
- Enable 2FA: This is the most important security measure you can take.
- Monitor your account activity: Regularly review your account activity for any suspicious activity.
- Report any suspicious activity: If you suspect that your account has been compromised, contact Xendit support immediately.

By taking these steps, you can help keep your Xendit account secure and protect your data and funds.
