title: "Top up balance"
---

Topping up your Xendit balance is how you add funds for payouts and other money-out transactions. It's important to know that top-ups are not for receiving payments from your customers.

How to top up

1. On your Dashboard, go to Balance
2. Click Top Up
3. Select your preferred top-up method
4. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the top-up

Always follow the top-up instructions carefully to prevent delays. Once you've made a transfer, you can check its status by going to Dashboard → Balance History and filtering by Top Up. Only successful top-ups will appear here, and you'll receive a confirmation email when the funds are credited.

## Topping up with foreign currency

If you need to add funds to your Xendit account from a foreign currency account, you have two main options.

### Option 1: Foreign Currency Top-Up (USD to Local Balance)

This method is for funding your local balance directly from a USD account. With this option, you get to approve the exchange rate before the money is converted.

How it works:

1. Send a USD wire transfer to the designated Xendit bank account for your region
2. Email [help@xendit.co](mailto:help@xendit.co) with your proof of transfer, the USD amount, and your business name
3. Our team will send you a real-time exchange rate quote via email or WhatsApp. You must approve this quote within 24 hours.
4. Once approved, the funds will be converted and credited to your local balance

### Option 2: Cross-Border Top-Up to an IDR Balance

This method is used when you want to top up your IDR balance from a foreign currency account. You have two ways to do this:

| Method | How to transfer | Foreign exchange & settlement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Auto-converted | Send foreign currency to your dedicated IDR Top-up Virtual Account | The foreign exchange rate is determined by the bank and the funds are automatically converted and credited. |
| Manual Foreign Exchange | Send a USD wire transfer to the BNI USD account. | Email [help@xendit.co](mailto:help@xendit.co) to lock in a specific exchange rate before the funds are converted. |
