You can withdraw your funds from your Xendit balance to the bank accounts that you’ve added to Xendit. There are 2 main ways that you can withdraw:

- Manual withdrawals: Make withdrawals from your Balance page
- [Auto-Withdrawals](/balance/auto-withdrawal): Schedule recurring withdrawals to automate the process

Processing times for withdrawals will vary based on your chosen method, when the request is made, and the information you provide.

## How to make manual withdrawals

### Local withdrawals

From the Balance page, trigger a Withdrawal and select “Local Withdrawal”. Enter the withdrawal amount, select a registered withdrawal account, enter your PIN then submit the request.

1. Go to your Balance page
2. Click Withdraw
3. Select Local Withdrawal
4. Enter the amount you want to withdraw
5. Choose which bank account to send the funds to
6. Enter your PIN
7. Click Submit

### Cross-border withdrawals

1. Go to your Balance page
2. Click Withdraw
3. Select Cross-border Withdrawal
4. Choose which bank account to send the funds to
5. Enter the amount you want to withdraw (you'll see the exchange rate and the amount you’ll receive in your bank account)
6. If necessary, upload the underlying document\*
7. Confirm your withdrawal details
8. Click Submit

*\*Each cross-border withdrawal requires an underlying document for regulatory and partner compliance. A template is provided on the withdrawal form (clickDownload template) which you can use to fill in your information.*

### USD balance withdrawals (Philippines)

For Philippines-based merchants with a USD ledger balance, withdrawals are currently processed manually via email.

- How to request: Email [help@xendit.ph](mailto:help@xendit.ph) to initiate the request and receive the required withdrawal templates.
- Fees: A processing fee is deducted from your balance per request: $5.00 for local banks (USD or PHP) and $50.00 for overseas banks.
- SLA: Requests sent before 2:00 PM (GMT+8) are processed on a T+1 basis.
- Availability: Supported for most major local banks (e.g., BDO, BPI, Metrobank, etc.).

Transfer fees are applicable for cross-border withdrawals. See [here](https://www.xendit.co/en-id/products/cross-border-payments/) for details on fees.

## Tracking withdrawals

> This feature is not available for [Global Accounts](/get-started/global-account).

You can set up automated notifications of completed withdrawals from the Dashboard under Withdrawal Settings. Add up to 10 email addresses as recipients who will receive emails notifying them of completed withdrawals. This applies to both manual and Auto-Withdrawals.

You can also check the status of individual withdrawal transactions from the Balance tab. Use the Balance History tab and filter out Withdrawal transactions within the right duration. If the withdrawal shows up, it’s completed. Otherwise, repeat the same process on the Pending tab to locate the pending withdrawal.
